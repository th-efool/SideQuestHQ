# TikTok/Insta Campaign (multi-account)

## Goal

- High-volume testing of hooks + angles; drive to landing page

## Account network (plan)

- 1 official brand account (product + credibility + demos)
- 6–12 “angle” accounts (mix of AI faces + faceless theme pages)
    - Build-in-public accounts (2)
    - Productivity + science of learning accounts (2–4)
    - “Learn to code” accounts (2–4)
    - Study-with-me / routines accounts (0–2)

## Angle library

- “I can’t finish long playlists”
- “20 minutes/day study system”
- “ADHD-friendly learning structure”
- “How to stop doom-saving videos”
- “Make a daily plan from any playlist”

## Hook templates

- “If you save playlists but never finish them…”
- “I turned this playlist into a 14-day plan in 10 seconds…”
- “Stop trying to ‘get motivated’. Do this instead…”

## Posting cadence

- Official: 1–2/day
- Others: 2–3/day/account (batch generate + schedule)

## Angle accounts (what they post + what they link)

- Build in public
    - Content: progress clips, “what we learned building X”, experiments, behind-the-scenes
    - CTA: follow + waitlist (occasional), no hard sell
- Productivity / science of learning
    - Content: attention, habit formation, active recall, spacing, “how to finish what you start”
    - CTA: “free playlist plan” / “template” → then soft link
- Learn to code
    - Content: beginner coding routines, project-based learning, “how I’d learn X in 30 days”
    - CTA destination: a topic-specific page on our site (e.g., “Agentic AI cohort” page) rather than the main homepage, so it reads like a normal resource link
    - Notes: keep it value-first; avoid implying you’re an unrelated third-party user; don’t use fake testimonials or impersonate real people

## Compliance / safety

- No fake testimonials or impersonation
- Don’t claim to be a real customer; keep it “theme/persona” content
- Follow platform rules around synthetic media when applicable

## Tracking

- UTM per account + link hub
