# AGRAY — VISUAL CHARACTER SHEET

## First Impression

He is not conventionally flashy.

Nothing about him screams "chosen one."

Instead, he possesses the kind of face that people instinctively remember after walking away.

The first thing anyone notices is not his physique.

Not his clothing.

Not even his attractiveness.

It is **his eyes.**

They never seem restless.

They simply watch.

---

# Overall Build

Height:  
Around **181–184 cm (5'11"–6'0")**

Build:

Athletic without appearing bulky.

Looks like someone whose body has been built through work rather than bodybuilding.

Wide shoulders.

Strong back.

Dense forearms.

Lean waist.

Powerful legs.

His body should always look functional.

Never exaggerated.

Never superhero proportions.

Think:

> someone who could climb mountains, wrestle, carry loads, and fight for hours.

Rather than:

> someone designed to flex.

His physique should become gradually denser through cultivation instead of dramatically larger.

---

# Face Structure

Strong masculine face.

Not soft.

Not overly sharp.

Balanced.

### Jaw

Wide.

Angular.

Very defined.

Especially visible when viewed from three-quarter angles.

### Chin

Square.

Solid.

Projects confidence without looking exaggerated.

### Cheekbones

Moderately high.

Become more visible during serious expressions due to slight facial tension.

### Forehead

Medium height.

Mostly hidden beneath hair.

---

# Skin

Medium warm brown.

Healthy.

Outdoor complexion.

Not pale.

Not heavily tanned.

As cultivation advances:

skin becomes smoother,

clearer,

almost unnaturally refined,

while retaining its natural tone.

No porcelain fantasy skin.

Still feels human.

---

# Eyes (Most Important)

These define the character.

## Shape

Deep-set.

Almond shaped.

Slightly hooded upper eyelids.

Horizontal orientation.

Never wide-open.

Never rounded.

They naturally appear focused.

---

## Iris

Pure black.

Not dark brown.

Not reflective gray.

True black.

The iris almost blends into the pupil.

This creates an unsettling effect.

The viewer struggles to distinguish where one ends and the other begins.

---

## Expression

His eyes should never look angry by default.

Instead they project:

- observation
    
- calculation
    
- restraint
    
- patience
    
- silent judgment
    

People mistake this for intimidation.

In reality:

he is simply paying attention.

---

## Intensity

The intensity comes from stillness.

Not from glaring.

His gaze rarely darts around.

When he looks at someone,

it feels like

he has already noticed everything worth noticing.

His eyes should almost never squint dramatically.

Instead,

his eyelids lower slightly,

creating quiet pressure.

---

## Eyebrows

Thick.

Naturally straight.

Slight downward angle toward the nose.

Never overly groomed.

They help create his permanently serious appearance.

---

# Nose

Straight bridge.

Medium width.

Defined tip.

Masculine proportions.

No exaggerated sharp anime nose.

---

# Lips

Medium thickness.

Lower lip slightly fuller.

Normally relaxed.

Rarely smiles broadly.

Most expressions are:

tiny corner movement

or

slight tightening.

---

# Hair

## Color

No blue tint.

No brown highlights.

Pure black.

---

## Texture

Naturally thick.

Dense.

Soft curls.

Loose curls rather than tight coils.

Hair should have volume,

especially on top.

## Style

Medium length.

Sides:

short but not skin fade.

# Default Posture

This is extremely important.

He rarely leans excessively.

Rarely gestures.

Stands evenly balanced.

Weight distributed naturally.

---

# Walking Style

Measured pace.

Never rushed.

---

# Facial Expressions

Default:

Neutral.

Observation.

Thinking.

When surprised:

eyes change before the mouth.

When angry:

jaw tightens,

eyes become stiller,

voice becomes quieter.

Never exaggerated anime expressions.

Emotion is communicated through subtle muscular tension.

---

# Combat Presence

The greatest visual change is not muscles.

It is stillness.

When battle begins:

his breathing slows.

Blink rate decreases.

Eyes lock.

Entire body becomes economical.

No unnecessary flourishes.

Every movement should feel inevitable.

Like someone solving a problem rather than winning a fight.

---

# Visual Evolution Through Cultivation

His evolution should mirror your cultivation philosophy, where increasing recursive internal consistency transforms the Self, Mandala, and presence rather than simply making the body larger.

### Early Stain

- Human
    
- Calm but youthful
    
- Thick black curls
    
- Clear skin
    
- Quiet intensity
    
- Physically athletic
    

### Brand

- More defined facial structure
    
- Denser musculature
    
- Greater stillness
    
- Eyes appear heavier
    
- Movements become instinctively efficient
    

### Carving

- Presence becomes unsettling
    
- Expression grows increasingly unreadable
    
- Blink frequency decreases
    
- People instinctively avoid prolonged eye contact
    

### Throne

- Feels unnaturally composed
    
- Crowds unconsciously make space around him
    
- His gaze alone alters the emotional atmosphere
    
- Every panel should make him feel visually "anchored" while others seem subtly reactive
    

### Abyss & Mahakala

Do **not** redesign him into an alien or monster.

The horror comes from recognition.

He still looks like the same man readers met in chapter one.

The difference is that every human feature has become impossibly refined, stable, and internally coherent. His stillness itself becomes oppressive, echoing how high-level cultivators overwrite the emotional and instinctual state of their surroundings.

# Manga Art Style Guide

Your art style should sit between realism and stylization—not hyper-realistic, not cartoonish.

### Influences

- _The Climber_ — grounded anatomy and expressive body language
    
- _Vagabond_ — textured line work and emotional stillness
    
- _The Ravages of Time_ — intelligent facial acting and historical atmosphere
    
- _Sun-Ken Rock_ — dense physical presence
    
- _Kingdom_ (early arcs) — readable action and military scale
    

## Anatomy

- Real human proportions
    
- Slight heroic exaggeration only
    
- Functional musculature
    
- Strong skeletal landmarks (jaw, collarbones, forearms, hands)
    

## Faces

- Individual facial structures for every major character
    
- Expression through eyes and subtle muscle shifts
    
- No oversized anime eyes
    
- Defined eyelids, noses, and lips
    

## Eyes

The eyes are the emotional engine.

Heavy upper eyelids.

Dense eyelashes only where anatomically natural.

Dark irises.

Strong shadows beneath the brow ridge.

The gaze should communicate more than dialogue.

## Hair

Hair should move as grouped strands, not hundreds of thin spikes.

Natural curls with believable weight and volume.

Never overly glossy.

## Clothing

Historically inspired by 8th–10th century India.

Layered cotton, linen, wrapped garments, practical robes, leather belts, and weathered fabrics rather than fantasy armor. Clothing should feel lived in and grounded in the politically fragmented, temple-centered world you've built.

## Line Work

- Clean structural outlines
    
- Fine interior detail
    
- Heavy blacks for dramatic contrast
    
- Dense shadows instead of excessive cross-hatching
    

## Lighting

Naturalistic.

Oil lamps.

Moonlight.

Monsoon skies.

Temple firelight.

Soft daylight.

Use light to sculpt faces rather than simply illuminate them.

## Action

Combat should feel weighty.

Every strike should show:

- balance shift
    
- hip rotation
    
- planted feet
    
- follow-through
    

Speed comes from timing and efficiency—not motion blur alone.

## Atmosphere

Your manga should constantly communicate pressure.

Even in quiet scenes.

Empty temples.

Wet stone.

Morning fog.

Oil lamps.

Ash drifting through the air.

Long pauses.

Silent eye contact.

Readers should feel the same psychological weight that defines your cultivation system: power is conveyed through stillness, internal consistency, and presence before it is ever shown through spectacle.
