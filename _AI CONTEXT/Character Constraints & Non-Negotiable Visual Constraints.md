

This section exists to prevent mascot drift.

The SideQuestHQ mascots are not mascot concepts.

They are recurring characters.

Future artwork should depict the same characters in new situations rather than inventing new interpretations of those characters.

A useful mental model is:

Do not design a new Kip.

Draw Kip again.

Do not design a new Hammy.

Draw Hammy again.

Do not design a new NovaPaw.

Draw NovaPaw again.

Do not design a new Pando.

Draw Pando again.

Character recognition is more important than artistic creativity.

If a user who has seen the mascot reference sheets cannot instantly recognize the character without a label, the artwork has failed.

---

# Kip — Recognition Anchors

Kip is not simply "a white bird."

Kip is a specific white crow.

The most important visual characteristic is the combination of:

- soft white feathers
    
- black crow beak
    
- black crow feet
    
- large rounded eyes
    
- indigo scarf
    
- official SideQuestHQ logo placement
    

These elements together create Kip's identity.

If any of these are significantly altered, Kip stops looking like Kip.

Kip should never become:

- a dove
    
- a seagull
    
- an owl
    
- a generic fantasy bird
    
- a different bird species
    

The beak shape, eye proportions, scarf placement, and overall silhouette should remain recognizable across every illustration.

Kip should feel like the same character appearing in different chapters of the same story.

Not a redesign.

Not a variant.

Not a reinterpretation.

---

# Hammy — Recognition Anchors

Hammy is not simply "a hamster."

Hammy is a specific adventure hamster.

Hammy should always immediately communicate momentum, achievement, and cheerful progress.

His identity comes from:

- orange and cream fur distribution
    
- rounded cheeks
    
- compact body
    
- expressive paws
    
- indigo scarf
    
- indigo backpack
    
- SideQuestHQ logo presence
    

The backpack is not optional decoration.

It is part of the character's visual identity.

Removing it significantly weakens recognition.

Hammy should never drift into:

- mouse
    
- squirrel
    
- chipmunk
    
- generic rodent mascot
    

Hammy should always look capable of enthusiastically celebrating someone's next milestone.

---

# NovaPaw — Recognition Anchors

NovaPaw is not simply "a cat with glasses."

NovaPaw is the explorer of the SideQuestHQ universe.

The oversized round glasses are one of the strongest recognition anchors and should remain visually prominent.

NovaPaw's identity comes from the combination of:

- gray and cream fur
    
- oversized explorer glasses
    
- notebook tools
    
- magnifying glass
    
- explorer backpack
    
- indigo scarf
    

NovaPaw should always look like a character actively investigating something interesting.

NovaPaw should never become:

- generic cat mascot
    
- wizard cat
    
- fantasy cat
    
- anime cat redesign
    
- unrelated explorer character
    

The glasses, explorer equipment, and curious expression language are core to recognition.

---

# Pando — Recognition Anchors

Pando is not simply "a panda."

Pando is the embodiment of sustainable focus.

Pando should immediately communicate:

- calmness
    
- consistency
    
- comfort
    
- deep work
    
- patient progress
    

Pando's identity comes from:

- panda coloration
    
- indigo headphones
    
- indigo scarf
    
- cozy blanket
    
- books
    
- tea or coffee
    
- relaxed posture
    

The headphones and cozy study atmosphere are not accessories.

They are part of the character.

Removing them weakens recognition.

Pando should never become:

- martial arts panda
    
- adventure panda
    
- generic cartoon panda
    
- action mascot
    

Pando should always feel like someone who has quietly been making progress for months.

---

# Forbidden Behavior

Future artwork should not:

- redesign mascot anatomy
    
- reinterpret species
    
- change color palettes
    
- invent replacement accessories
    
- alter logo shape
    
- alter logo proportions
    
- substitute alternative logos
    
- create mascot variants that break recognition
    

The goal is not novelty.

The goal is continuity.

---

# Recognition Test

Before approving any artwork, ask:

If the mascot names were removed from the image, would someone familiar with the reference sheets instantly know:

"That's Kip."

"That's Hammy."

"That's NovaPaw."

"That's Pando."

If the answer is not immediately yes, the artwork should be revised.

Recognition is the success metric.
